01 Understanding the brief

Recruitment doesn't stop at the catchment boundary. Neither can safety monitoring.

Patients who qualify for your trial often live too far to attend the centre for every timepoint. The follow-up still has to happen, adverse events still have to be caught early — with a photo the investigator can actually see — and protocol-driven data still has to land on time.

DISTANCE

Follow-up without the journey

Distant patients miss visits and drop out. Assessments must reach them where they are, timed to the protocol, not the postcode.

SAFETY

Adverse events between visits

A rash or swelling can appear days before the next contact. The investigator needs to see it quickly, not at the next round.

DATA

Timepoints that hold

Disease-specific and welfare assessments lose value when they slip. Missed-timepoint drift becomes protocol deviation.

07 Regulatory & compliance

A UK trial, scoped honestly. No device pathway by design.

Geography is the UK, so the frame is MHRA plus UK data-protection law — no EU MDR, no US 21 CFR Part 11. Compliance is a design constraint, not paperwork added at the end.

The design rule that keeps this off the device pathway

The app routes information. The investigator makes every clinical call.

A patient reports a symptom; the app flags it and sends it to a human. It never interprets severity or grades the event itself — so it stays out of medical-device classification under the UK Medical Devices Regulations 2002. This boundary is held through change control.

09 What we need from you

The few open items that sharpen the estimate.

None of these block discovery — but answering them lets us firm up scope, licensing, and the timeline.

Are the disease-specific and quality-of-life assessments licensed/validated instruments? Can you share the list?
BYOD is confirmed — what minimum phone spec and photo-quality floor do AE images need, and how are patients below it handled?
Are protocol timepoints fixed per study or per patient arm, and what is the source of truth for the visit schedule?
For two-way messaging, who is clinically responsible for monitoring and responding, and within what window?
AE photos: what retention period, and must images be de-identified before storage or review?
What is the escalation pathway for a red-flag event — who is notified, in what order, with what fallback?
Confirm the role list and per-role permissions, and whether AE e-signature is a Must or a Should.
Expected patient and concurrent-study volumes — and is a pilot study available early for validation?
